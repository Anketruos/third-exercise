Ingredients and Varieties

At its core, bubble tea is made with a tea base, which can be black, green, or herbal tea. The tea is usually sweetened and mixed with milk or fruit flavors, creating a rich and refreshing beverage. The signature component of bubble tea is the tapioca pearls, which are small, round balls made from tapioca starch derived from cassava root. These pearls are cooked until they achieve a chewy texture, making them a fun addition to the drink.

Bubble tea comes in numerous variations, allowing for a wide range of flavors and textures. Some popular options include:

Classic milk tea Tiger milk tea Thai milk tea Taro milk tea Lychee fruit tea Blueberry bubble tea Caramel bubble tea
  1. Milk Tea: A classic bubble tea made with black or green tea, milk, and sugar.
  2. Fruit Tea: A refreshing blend of tea with fresh fruit or fruit-flavored syrups.
  3. Smoothie-style: A thicker version that combines tea, fruit, and ice, blended into a smoothie-like consistency.
  4. Flavored Pearls: In addition to traditional tapioca pearls, some shops offer flavored or colored pearls made from different ingredients.
